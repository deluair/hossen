Background

A bit about me

I studied chemical engineering at BUET, then spent several years in banking regulation and financial intelligence in Bangladesh. I moved to the U.S. for a Ph.D. in Economics at the University of Tennessee, where I focused on trade dynamics and exchange rate pass-through. Now I work as a postdoctoral researcher in agricultural and resource economics, collaborating on international trade, food security, and macroeconomic modeling. Along the way I picked up full-stack engineering because the problems I work on need working software, not just papers.
